# I Almost Paid $240 for Airport Parking. Then I Found This Weirdly Useful Comparison Tool.

Last month I was planning a week-long trip out of JFK. Flights booked, bags ready, everything sorted — until I remembered I needed to park my car somewhere for seven days.

I checked the official JFK long-term parking rate: $35 a day. For a week, that's $245. I almost accepted it.

Then I did what anyone does: opened Google and searched "cheap JFK airport parking." I found the usual suspects — a few individual lot websites, a couple of booking platforms that wanted me to create an account before showing prices. And then I found something called [AirportMatrix](https://airportmatrix.com).

It was just a simple page. Search bar at the top. I typed "JFK" and hit enter.

Within seconds, I was staring at 15 different parking options, all sorted by price, ranging from $9.99 to $34.99 a day. The table showed each lot's distance from the airport, whether they had a shuttle, and what type of parking it was.

I clicked on the cheapest one — [ARB Parking at $17.73/day](https://airportmatrix.com/airport/jfk/arb-parking-jfk-airport). The page told me exactly what I needed to know. It's 0.8 miles from JFK. The shuttle runs every 15 minutes. Free cancellation up to the minute you check in. And someone had clearly summarized what hundreds of people said about the place — the shuttle is punctual, the staff is friendly, the prices are legitimate.

I'd never seen a parking site that had actual review summaries before. It felt less like a comparison tool and more like asking a friend who flies out of JFK all the time where they park.

One detail that stood out: the page showed me real stats about the lot. 1,101 travelers had parked there. It was rated 4.7 out of 5. There was even a bar chart breaking down ratings for staff, facility, location, and safety — not just a generic star rating.

I checked two other options just to be sure. [Another lot nearby](https://airportmatrix.com/airport/jfk) was $9.99 a day but didn't have a shuttle — you'd need to take a rideshare to the terminal. There was an indoor parking option at $29.99. All of this was on the same page, no signups, no redirects to third-party booking sites.

I booked ARB Parking through the link on the page. Total for seven days: $124.11. I saved $120 compared to parking at the airport.

Here's what I think is actually going on under the hood: someone built a tool that pulls real-time parking data, runs it through AI to generate descriptions and review summaries, and presents everything in a clean comparison table. It's not another booking site trying to upsell you. It's literally just a comparison engine.

The closest thing I can compare it to is Google Flights, but for airport parking. You search, you compare, you click through to book. No account required. No fees.

Is it perfect? Not quite. Some smaller airports only have a few options listed. The review summaries are AI-generated, which occasionally reads slightly unnatural — though every claim I checked was backed up by the actual reviews. And it only covers US airports right now.

But for what it is — a free, fast way to compare airport parking prices without wading through a dozen booking sites — it's surprisingly good. I've bookmarked it for my next trip.
